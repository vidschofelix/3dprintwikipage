# Disclaimer

The information on **3DPrint.Wiki** is contributed by volunteers from around the world. It is provided **as-is, without warranty of any kind**, express or implied.

3D printers involve **high temperatures, moving parts, and mains voltage**. Any modification you perform on a printer — wiring, firmware flashing, MOSFET installation, mechanical changes — is done at your own risk and may damage your hardware, void your warranty, or cause fire or injury.

Always:

- Read the relevant manufacturer documentation.
- Verify wiring with a multimeter before powering on.
- Use appropriate gauge wire and rated connectors for the current you are switching.
- Keep a fire extinguisher near any unattended printer.
- Never leave a printer running unattended unless you have actively engineered for it (thermal runaway protection, enclosure, smoke detection).

Pages flagged as *historical reference* may describe procedures or firmware that are no longer supported or safe by current standards. Treat them as archaeology, not as instructions.
